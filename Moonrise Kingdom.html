Moonrise Kingdom , released in 2012, is a quirky coming-of-age story about
two kids who fall madly in love and run away from home.
Shortly after their disappearance a catastrophic storm approaches the island
and the adults must find them before dangers strikes.
This offbeat, deadpan film, directed by Wes Anderson, was one of the
first films I studied and wrote about. The color, the camera movements,
the simple yet profound dialogue all made this movie stand out as something that
many filmmakers still study to this day. I believe that the first time I saw this film, I was actually
still a teenager. The impact it had on me as a child was completely different from the impact
it had on rastically impacted how I viewed the boundaries of filmmaking.
